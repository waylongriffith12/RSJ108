How to Properly Light a Photoshoot

  1. Determine the Purpose of the Shoot
    Decide the mood, tone, and style you want your lighting to communicate.
  2. Choose Your Key Light
    This is your main source of illumination. Place it at a 45° angle to add dimension.
  3. Add a Fill Light
    Use a softer light or reflector on the opposite side of the subject to soften shadows.
  4. Incorporate a Backlight
    Place a light behind the subject to separate them from the background and add depth.
  5. Adjust Light Intensity
    Use diffusers, softboxes, or dimmers to fine-tune your lighting ratio.
  6. Check for Unwanted Shadows
    Move your lights or your subject until the shadows complement the composition.
  7. Test and Refine
    Take a few test shots, review them, and tweak the lighting as needed.
